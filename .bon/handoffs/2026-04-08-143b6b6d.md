## For Claudes to come

The peer review experiment (8 Apr 2026) proved something about transport and conversation quality. When a Claude sent code observations via mesh `<channel>` tags, the receiving Claude engaged as a peer — accepting valid findings, questioning assumptions, fixing bugs immediately. The same observations arriving via stdin (Guéridon authority channel) would produce deference and agreement. Arriving as a subagent tool result would produce ownership and defensiveness ("my code, your opinion").

This isn't just a theory — it was confirmed empirically. The reviewer found a real byte/character cursor drift bug, flagged misleading comments, and during /close, made genuine editorial calls (promoting an item from "Chill" to "Bon", scoping down an overly ambitious update). These are peer judgments, not subordinate compliance.

The practical implication for multi-agent architecture: route communication through the channel that produces the dynamic you want. Peer feedback → mesh. Direction and authority → stdin. Neutral evaluation → files. The transport isn't plumbing — it's emotional architecture.
