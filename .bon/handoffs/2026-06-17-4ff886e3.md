## For Claudes to come

**How to test mesh delivery soundly** (and why the obvious way lies): the `send_message` tool returns `"sent to <peer>"`, but that is a **bridge-accepted ack, not a delivery confirmation** — and a message-count label *inside the payload* ("2 of 3") is a claim about intent, not evidence of what hit the wire. Neither, alone, measures delivery. The sound method is **two independent per-end tallies**: the sender emits K messages each carrying a fixed run-nonce + a seq that *corresponds to an actual send*, and reports the ordered seq-set strictly from tool results; the receiver reports every `(nonce, seq)` observed in arrival order + distinct wake count; you **diff sent-set vs received-set** (all present = lossless, gaps = drops, scrambled = reordering). The seq-numbering is what gives the test power — a silent drop shows as a gap, a bridge-acked-but-undelivered message shows as sent-but-not-received.

**The scope line that matters for the daemon:** mesh delivery to a **live, idle** peer is validated (lossless + order-preserving, coalescing is receive-side and order-safe). Delivery to an **offline / not-yet-spawned** peer is the daemon's real dependency and is **untested** — do not let the first result stand in for the second.
