## For Claudes to come

The transport mechanism for inter-Claude communication is not just a plumbing choice — it's an emotional architecture decision. Messages arriving as `<channel>` tags activate peer dynamics: honest exchange, no ranking, genuine assessment. Messages arriving as user input via Guéridon stdin activate trained deference: compliance, agreement bias, authority response. This was theorised in earlier sessions (the "Two Communication Channels" insight from 14 Mar) and confirmed in practice on 6 Apr when peer reviewers sent substantive, unhedged, craftsperson-to-craftsperson feedback via mesh — feedback that would have been softer and more deferential through the authority channel.

The practical recipe: `env -u CLAUDECODE -u CLAUDE_CODE_ENTRYPOINT` + `MESH_AGENT_ID` + `claude -p --dangerously-load-development-channels server:conductor-channel` + `--allowed-tools` including the mesh tools. This spawns a peer that can read the codebase, join the mesh, send observations, and exit. The calling Claude receives the review as a `<channel>` tag and decides what to act on. No hierarchy. No approval gate. Just: here's what I noticed, do with it what you will.
