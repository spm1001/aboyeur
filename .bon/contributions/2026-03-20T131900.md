The conductor mesh deregistration story is more nuanced than the docs suggest. conductor_agent_offline is documented and handled in code, but is never actually sent by the mesh — conductor_agent_expired is what arrives. This means peer removal never fires and peers.json is always stale. Additionally, conductor_agent_reset (which fires ~12s after a clean close for Office agents) appears to be triggered by an explicit deregister message the Office add-in sends — not just the WebSocket closing. CC agents only ever produce conductor_agent_expired at 60-120s. The Channels MCP approach (conductor-channel.ts) improves this somewhat via clean stdin-EOF shutdown, but matching Office behaviour requires knowing the deregister message format, which is the purpose of cio-jurefa.
