The conductor bridge must send `{ "type": "deregister", "_agent_id": "<id>" }` before closing its WebSocket to get the fast disconnect path (~12s `conductor_agent_reset` on peers). Without this, peers see `conductor_agent_offline` (which is a no-op — empty handler in the bundle) followed by `conductor_agent_expired` after 60-120s. The current `ConductorBridge.close()` method just calls `ws.close()` without deregistering — this is the fix for aby-cahagi's "good guests on the bridge" requirement. Additionally, the bridge server has two separate endpoints: `/v2/conductor/{uuid}` for the mesh and `/office/{uuid}` for Cowork pairing. Connecting to the wrong one (as happened during the 21 Mar live session) puts you on the pairing protocol instead of the conductor mesh. The aboyeur bridge already uses the correct endpoint. Finally, Office Claudes confirmed via direct interview that they have no visibility of the deregister mechanism — it happens in the transport layer. Both PowerPoint and Excel agents independently stated "no graceful goodbye protocol" from their perspective, while the bundle code clearly shows `wdt.close()` sending the deregister behind the scenes.
